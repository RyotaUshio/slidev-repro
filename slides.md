Let $r = \mathrm{Pr}(A = 0)$ and $0 \leq \delta < 1/2$ be a constant. Assume:

- $\eta(x, a) = \delta$ or $1 - \delta$ for all $x \in \mathcal{X}, \ a \in \mathcal{A}$.
- Data bias is introduced by the under-representation+label bias model, whose parameters $\beta_p, \beta_n, \nu$ satisfy:
$$
\begin{gathered}
(1-r)(1-2 \delta)+r\left((1-\delta) \beta_p(1-2 \nu)-\delta \beta_n\right)>0, \\
(1-r)(1-2 \delta)+r\left((1-\delta) \beta_n-\delta \beta_p(1-2 \nu)\right)>0.
\end{gathered}
$$
